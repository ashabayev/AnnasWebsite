Life Out of the House: Covid19 | 360 film

May 2020

Filmed in three locations during the Covid19 Pandemic. O'Hare International airport, Boston State House, and the Boston Commons Duck Pond.

While setting up for the first shot at the Boston State House, a couple of interesting things happened that weren't caught on camera. First, I saw some protestors walking away in full American flag attire. Next, saw a man on a longboard playing movie dialogue through a loudspeaker while wearing a gas mask. And then saw a Trump supporter recording people and intentionally going in their personal space and breaking social distancing rules. Here is a link to the protests that happened 3 hours before this footage was taken.

In my videos, I was able to capture some of the ways that people interact during this time. In the airport and Boston, not everyone is wearing a mask, and if they are, often times I've seen it not being worn properly. In the videos I took, it could be easily mistaken for a quiet part of the day if it weren't for many people wearing masks. People don't really seem to be displaying worry in the public spaces I took footage in and in the Boston commons, I barely saw people walk out of the way. This is quite a contrast to my suburban home town, where if someone sees you on their path, they tend to cross the street to avoid getting close.

I worked with two different 360 cameras for this project. One being the Insta360 Evo and the other being the Insta360 Pro. Through this process, I realized that the hardward for Insta360 is very nice and dependable however, they are lacking on the software side. I had to download to seperate programs to stitch the footage, one for each camera. The UI wasn't even consistent accross the two applications. It would have been better if they could have one software to unite all the cameras to prevent conffusion and frusteration. To edit in premiere, I believe I had to download two seperate plugins as well. But again, while their software is lacking, Insta360's hardware is on point.
